Nutritional Value
Calculation



Most of our nutritional values at HomeMeals come from the USDA FoodData Central (https://fdc.nal.usda.gov/fdc-app.html#/) and the Food Composition Table of Central America ( https://www.incap.int/mesocaribefoods/dmdocuments/TablaCAlimentos.pdf ), which are comprehensive databases of nutritional information managed by the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) and the Institute of Nutrition of Central America and Panama (INCAP), respectively. Within these databases, details about the nutritional composition of a wide variety of raw or processed foods can be provided and are used by healthcare professionals, nutritionists, researchers, and the general public to obtain accurate information about the nutritional content of foods.

Additionally, we gather information from nutritional value labels of processed products when necessary.

Furthermore, we collaborate with certified nutritionists, graduates of the University of Medical Sciences, and incorporated into the respective College of Nutrition Professionals, to ensure the accuracy of our database.

It is important to remember that these calculations are estimates based on the products used and may vary depending on the accuracy of the available information, the precision of the measurements used, geographical location, and the type of brands used. Therefore, if you have specific nutritional needs or are following a particular diet, it is advisable to consult with a nutritionist for more accurate and personalized guidance

The methodology used to calculate the nutritional value of each of the created recipes is as follows:

  • Ingredient Compilation: All recipe ingredients, including specific quantities, are recorded.
  • Nutritional Information Source Consultation: Our food databases are used to obtain precise nutritional information per gram for each ingredient.
  • Ingredient Weighing: Each ingredient is accurately weighed, including both solid and liquid ingredients.
  • Calculations: With the nutritional information and quantities of each ingredient, necessary calculations are performed. The unit of 1 gram per ingredient is used as a reference, and nutritional values are multiplied according to the amount necessary for 1 portion. The formula used is as follows: Total Nutritional Value=Grams of Ingredient×Nutritional ValueTotal Nutritional Value=Grams of Ingredient×Nutritional Value This is a mathematical representation of how the quantities of ingredients and their respective nutritional values combine to obtain the total nutritional value of the recipe.
  • Information Recording: All calculations and values obtained are documented in the databases. A simple example of how the nutritional value is calculated in one of the recipes would be as follows: if a recipe requires 10 grams of butter per serving and the USDA indicates that the calorie content in 1 gram of butter is 7.17, the calories are multiplied by the grams, resulting in a final value of 71.7 calories from that ingredient per serving.
